# Labor & Employment Laws in India: Employee Rights

Employee-employer relationships are regulated by various Central and State acts, including the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, Payment of Wages Act, 1936, and state-specific Shops and Establishments Acts.

## 1. Right to Timely Payment of Salary
- Wages must be paid before the **7th day** of the following month (for establishments with fewer than 1000 employees) or the 10th day.
- Unpaid salary is a direct violation of the Payment of Wages Act. Employees can file complaints with the Labor Commissioner.

## 2. Termination of Employment & Notice Period
- **Employment Contract**: Termination must comply with the terms of the employment contract and the state Shops and Establishments Act.
- **Notice Period**: Typically, 30 days' notice or 1 month's salary in lieu of notice is mandatory for termination without cause.
- **Retrenchment & Layoff**: Under the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, retrenchment requires payment of 15 days' average pay for every completed year of continuous service (Retrenchment Compensation).
- **Illegal Dismissal**: If an employee is terminated arbitrarily (e.g., during pregnancy, sickness, or without any explanation/inquiry), they can sue for wrongful termination.

## 3. Full & Final Settlement (FnF)
- The employer must clear all dues (salary, gratuity, leave encashment, bonus) within **2 working days** of the employee's resignation or termination under the new labor codes, or as specified by local state laws (usually within 30-45 days).

## 4. Working Hours, Leave, and Overtime
- **Standard Hours**: Maximum 8-9 hours per day or 48 hours per week.
- **Overtime**: Any work beyond standard hours entitles the employee to overtime wages at **double the normal rate** of wages.
- **Leaves**: Employees are entitled to Casual Leave (CL), Sick Leave (SL), and Earned/Privilege Leave (EL/PL).

## 5. Dispute Resolution Process
1. **Send a Legal Notice**: Send a demand notice to the employer requesting outstanding dues or reinstatement.
2. **Labor Commissioner**: File a complaint of exploitation or non-payment of wages at the local Labor Commissioner office.
3. **Conciliation**: The Labor Officer conducts conciliation meetings between employer and employee.
4. **Labor Court**: If conciliation fails, the dispute is referred to the Labor Court/Tribunal for adjudication.
