# Consumer Protection Act, 2019: Rights, Redressal, and Procedures

The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 (CPA) governs consumer rights, unfair trade practices, misleading advertisements, and product liability in India. 

## 1. Six Core Consumer Rights
1. **Right to Safety**: Protection against marketing of goods and services which are hazardous to life and property.
2. **Right to be Informed**: Right to know the quality, quantity, potency, purity, standard, and price of goods or services.
3. **Right to Choose**: Assured access to a variety of goods and services at competitive prices.
4. **Right to be Heard**: Assurance that consumers' interests will receive due consideration at appropriate forums.
5. **Right to Seek Redressal**: Right to seek legal remedies against unfair trade practices or exploitation.
6. **Right to Consumer Awareness**: Right to acquire knowledge and skill to be an informed consumer.

## 2. Who is a "Consumer"?
A person who buys goods or hires/avails of any service for consideration (payment), including online and offline transactions. It does NOT include a person who obtains goods for resale or commercial purposes.

## 3. Redressal Forums (Three-Tier Pecuniary Jurisdiction)
- **District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (District Commission)**: Claims up to ₹50 Lakhs.
- **State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (State Commission)**: Claims between ₹50 Lakhs and ₹2 Crores.
- **National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (National Commission)**: Claims exceeding ₹2 Crores.

## 4. How to File a Complaint
1. **Send a Legal Notice**: Before filing a case, send a formal written notice to the vendor/service provider explaining the defect/deficiency, giving them 15 days to resolve the issue.
2. **e-Daakhil Portal**: Complaints can be filed online via the e-Daakhil portal (edaakhil.nic.in) or physically at the local commission where the complainant resides or where the transaction occurred.
3. **Time Limit**: A complaint must be filed within **2 years** from the date on which the cause of action arose (e.g., date of purchase, date of service failure).
4. **Fees**: No court fees for claims up to ₹5 Lakhs. Small nominal fees apply for higher amounts.
5. **No Lawyer Required**: A consumer can represent themselves (party-in-person) without hiring a lawyer.

## 5. Key Remedies & Relief
- Replacement of defective goods or refund of the price.
- Removal of deficiency in service.
- Compensation for loss, injury, or mental harassment.
- Discontinuation of unfair trade practices.
