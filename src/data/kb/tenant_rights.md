# Tenant Rights & Landlord-Tenant Disputes in India

Landlord-tenant relationships are governed by state-specific Rent Control Acts and the Model Tenancy Act, 2021 (MTA), which is being adopted across states.

## 1. Rental Agreement
- **Written Agreement**: It is mandatory to have a written rental agreement registered with the Rent Authority.
- **Validity & Renewal**: Standard agreements are usually for 11 months to avoid complex registration rules. If it exceeds 12 months, registration is compulsory.

## 2. Security Deposit Rules
- **Cap on Deposit**: Under the Model Tenancy Act, the security deposit is capped at a maximum of **2 months' rent** for residential properties and 6 months' rent for non-residential properties.
- **Refund Policy**: The landlord must refund the security deposit at the time of taking over vacant possession of the premises, after making reasonable deductions for unpaid rent or utility bills. The landlord cannot make arbitrary deductions for "wear and tear" or painting.

## 3. Protection Against Unlawful Eviction
- A landlord **cannot** evict a tenant without a valid order from the Rent Court or Rent Tribunal.
- Valid grounds for eviction:
  - Non-payment of rent for two consecutive months.
  - Subletting the property without the landlord's written consent.
  - Damage to the property causing material depreciation.
  - The landlord needs the premises for their own occupation (genuine requirement).
- **Notice Period**: The landlord must serve a formal written notice (typically 1 to 2 months) specifying the grounds before starting eviction proceedings.

## 4. Maintenance & Repair Responsibilities
- **Landlord Responsibility**: Structural repairs, whitewashing, external electrical wiring, and plumbing leaks.
- **Tenant Responsibility**: General upkeep, bulb replacement, minor repairs, and maintaining cleanliness.
- If a landlord refuses to carry out structural repairs, the tenant can do it and deduct the cost from the rent, after giving notice.

## 5. Right to Essential Services
- A landlord **cannot** cut off essential supplies or services such as electricity, water supply, elevator, or passage to the tenant under any circumstances (even if rent is unpaid).
- If cut off, the tenant can approach the Rent Authority, which can order immediate restoration and penalize the landlord.
