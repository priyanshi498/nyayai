# Police Procedures, FIRs, and Constitutional Rights in India

Police operations, complaints, and arrests are governed by the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC) / Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS) and the Indian Penal Code (IPC) / Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS).

## 1. First Information Report (FIR)
- **What is an FIR?**: A written document prepared by the police when they receive information about the commission of a **cognizable offense** (serious crimes like theft, assault, murder, rape where police can arrest without a warrant).
- **Who can file?**: Anyone who knows about the commission of a cognizable offense (the victim, a witness, or even the police themselves).
- **Free Copy**: It is the legal right of the informant to receive a copy of the FIR free of cost immediately.

## 2. What to do if the Police Refuse to File an FIR
If a police officer-in-charge refuses to register an FIR:
1. **Superintendent of Police (SP)**: Send the complaint in writing by registered post to the concerned Superintendent of Police (SP) or Commissioner of Police. If satisfied, the SP will investigate or direct an officer to do so.
2. **Magistrate Complaint**: File a private criminal complaint under Section 156(3) CrPC / Section 175 BNSS before the local Judicial Magistrate, requesting the court to order the police to register the FIR and investigate.
3. **Zero FIR**: If an offense occurs outside the jurisdiction of a police station, they must still register the complaint (called a Zero FIR) and transfer it to the appropriate station. They cannot refuse filing based on jurisdiction.

## 3. Constitutional Rights Upon Arrest (Article 22 & CrPC/BNSS)
- **Right to Know Grounds**: The arrestee has the right to be informed of the reasons/grounds for their arrest immediately.
- **Right to Bail**: If arrested for a bailable offense, the police must inform the person that they are entitled to release on bail.
- **24-Hour Rule**: The arrested person must be produced before the nearest Judicial Magistrate within **24 hours** of arrest (excluding travel time). Detention beyond 24 hours without a magistrate's order is illegal.
- **Right to Legal Counsel**: The arrestee has the right to consult and be defended by a lawyer of their choice.
- **Medical Examination**: The arrested person has the right to be medically examined by a medical practitioner at the time of arrest to record any injuries.

## 4. Special Rights for Women
- **Arrest Timings**: A woman cannot be arrested after sunset (6 PM) and before sunrise (6 AM) except under exceptional circumstances and with prior permission of a Judicial Magistrate.
- **Female Officer**: A woman can only be arrested and searched by a **female police officer**.
- **Detention**: Women must be kept in separate lockups, not with male detainees.
